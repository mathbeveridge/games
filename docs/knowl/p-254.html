Let \(\cA = \{ X \in \cG \mid g(X)=0 \}\) and \(\cB = \{ Y \in \cG \mid g(X) \neq 0 \}\text{.}\) If \(X \in \cA\text{,}\) then no follower of \(X\) is in \(\cA\) (because otherwise \(g(X) \neq 0\)). In other words, every follower of \(X\) is in \(\cB\text{.}\) Meanwhile, if \(Y \in \cB\) then \(g(Y) \neq 0\text{.}\) This means that there is at least one follower of \(Y\) that is in \(\cA\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 1.3.6, \(\cA\) is the set of \(\cP\)-positions and \(\cB\) is the set of \(\cN\)-positions.
