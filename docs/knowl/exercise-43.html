Exercise 7.

This exercise guides you through the proof of Lemma \ref{lemma-loop}. Let \(G\) be a multigraph rooted at vertex \(x\) with a loop edge \(e\) incident with vertex \(y\text{.}\) Let \(H\) be the multigraph obtained by replacing this loop by a twig edge \(f\) from \(y\) to a new vertex \(z\text{.}\) You will show that the Green Hackenbush game position \(G \oplus H\) satisfies \(\g(G \oplus H) = 0\text{.}\)

  1. Suppose that Left hacks the loop \(e\) in \(G\text{.}\) What should Right do? How do you know that this results in a \Ppos?

  2. Suppose that Left hacks an edge in \(G\) that disconnects the loop \(e\) from the root of \(G\text{.}\) What should Right do?

  3. Use induction on the number of edges \(m\) in \(G\) to prove that if Left hacks an edge in \(G\) that does not disconnect the loop \(e\) from the root, then Right has a move in \(H\) to create a \Ppos. Your base case is \(m=0\text{,}\) in which \(G\) consists of a single root vertex \(x\) with no edges. (This means that for the base case, we have \(y=x\) since this is the only vertex in \(G\text{.}\))

  4. What if Left makes his first move in \(H\) instead of in \(G\text{?}\)

  5. Explain why \(\g(G \oplus H) = 0\) means that \(\g(G) = \g(H)\text{.}\)
