Theorem 3.5.1.

Suppose that we have games \(\cG_1, \cG_2, \ldots , \cG_n\) with respective Sprague-Grundy functions \(g_1, g_2, \ldots g_n\text{.}\) Then the sum of games

\begin{equation*} \cG_1 \oplus \cG_2 \oplus \cdots \oplus \cG_n \end{equation*}

has Sprague-Grundy function

\begin{equation*} g(X_1, X_2, \ldots, X_n) = g_1(X_1) \oplus g_2(X_2) \oplus \cdots \oplus g_n(X_n) \end{equation*}
