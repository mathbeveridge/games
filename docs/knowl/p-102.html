  1. Find the largest \(k\) such that \(2^k \leq n\)

  2. Repeat step 1 for the remainder \(n - 2^k\) until you reach 0.

  3. This gives you the expansion \(n= 2^{k_1} + 2^{k_2} + \cdots + 2^{k_r}\text{.}\)

  4. The binary representation of \(n\) consists of \(k_1+1\) digits. It has a 1 in the positions \(k_1, k_2, \ldots, k_r\text{,}\) where the rightmost position is indexed by 0. The remaining digits are each 0.
