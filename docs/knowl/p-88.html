In fact, the name Nimble is a clever play on words: the game of Nimble is actually Nim in disguised form! This masquerade is quite thin and easy to reveal. In Nim, we can reduce a heap of \(n\) beans to a heap of \(n-1, n-2, \ldots, 2, 1\) or 0 beans. In Nimble, we can move a coin from square \(n\) to square \(n-1, n-2, \ldots, 2, 1\) or off the board (onto "square 0"). So here is the disguise: the coins in a game of Nimble mark the sizes of the heaps in the corresponding game of Nim.
