In this problem, you will prove the Parity Principle of Theorem \ref{thm-parity}: the Sprague-Grundy value of any Lumberjack position has the same parity as the total number of edges. The key idea behind this proof is the following simple observation:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcccl} \mbox{even} + \mbox{even} &=& \mbox{odd} + \mbox{odd} &=& \mbox{even} \\ \mbox{even} + \mbox{odd} &=& \mbox{odd} + \mbox{even} &=& \mbox{odd} \\ \\ \mbox{even} \oplus \mbox{even} &=& \mbox{odd} \oplus \mbox{odd} &=& \mbox{even} \\ \mbox{even} \oplus \mbox{odd} &=& \mbox{odd} \oplus \mbox{even} &=& \mbox{odd} \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}

  1. Prove that the Parity Principle holds for \(k\)-tufts.

  2. Let \(G\) be a tree, and let \(m\) be the number vertices of degree greater than 2. Use induction on \(m\) to prove that the Parity Principle holds for \(G\text{.}\) You will need to use part (a) during your inductive step.

  3. Use part (b) to prove that if \(H\) is a forest, then the Parity Principle holds.
